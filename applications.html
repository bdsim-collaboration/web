Applications of BDSIM

Crystal Channeling with BDSIM

BDSIM provides detailed simulation capabilities for bent crystals used in collimation systems and other types of beam control. The code models processes such as channeling, dechanneling, volume capture, and volume reflection, enabling realistic studies of beam–crystal interactions. These effects are essential for assessing the performance of crystal-assisted collimation schemes in high-energy accelerators. Collimation Studies with BDSIM

BDSIM is widely used to simulate collimation systems and evaluate their cleaning performance in high-energy accelerators. In a typical setup, particles are tracked with a dedicated tracking code until they encounter a collimator, at which point BDSIM is used to model the detailed particle–matter interaction. Secondary particles produced, or primary particles surviving the interaction, are then passed back to the tracking code for continued tracking until they are absorbed or lost in the machine aperture.
